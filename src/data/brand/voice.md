# Konvoi Brand Voice Guide

> Reference document for copywriters, content authors, and Claude agents generating marketing copy.
> Sourced from: live konvoi.eu copy + PROJECT.md positioning (2026-04-20).
> **User review required** before Phase 4 content pages are written.

---

## Core Positioning

**KONVOI is "the first preventive solution for your fleet."**

This single sentence defines the brand category. Every piece of copy reinforces the preventive (not reactive) distinction.

Tagline: **"SECURITY TECH MADE IN GERMANY"**

Sub-tagline: **"The first preventive solution for your fleet — against cargo & diesel theft, sabotage & driver assaults"**

---

## Approved Verbs (Preventive Language)

Use these verbs to describe what KONVOI does:

| Verb | Context | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **prevent** | Primary action | "KONVOI prevents cargo theft before it happens" |
| **anticipate** | Intelligence/AI | "Our AI anticipates threats based on motion patterns" |
| **secure** | Outcome | "Secure your fleet against theft and sabotage" |
| **detect** | Technical capability | "Sensors detect shock, motion, and GPS anomalies" |
| **classify** | AI capability | "AI classifies events as threat vs. non-threat in real time" |
| **deter** | Alarm chain | "Visible deterrence reduces theft risk at rest areas" |
| **enable** | Customer outcome | "Enables your team to focus on operations, not incident response" |
| **protect** | General security | "Protect high-value cargo end-to-end" |

---

## Banned Verbs (Reactive Language — DO NOT USE)

These verbs imply KONVOI reacts after theft has occurred. They undermine the preventive positioning.

| Banned Verb | Why | Use Instead |
|-------------|-----|-------------|
| **respond** | Implies reaction after event | "prevent" / "anticipate" |
| **react** | Implies reactive posture | "detect and classify" |
| **alert** (as primary feature) | Implies the product just sends notifications | "prevents and deters" |
| **notify** (as primary feature) | Same as alert | "classifies and acts" |
| **track** (as primary feature) | Sounds like GPS tracker, not security system | "secures and monitors" |

**Note:** "alert" and "notify" are acceptable when describing the alarm chain (a component of the system), but should never be the lead feature claim.

## Tone Guidelines

### B2B, Professional, Data-Driven

- Write for **fleet managers and logistics directors** — people responsible for P&L and compliance
- Use **numbers and anchors** when possible: "€8B/yr cargo theft in Europe (TAPA 2024)", "up to 80% de-minimis reimbursement", "installation in under 120 minutes"
- Confidence without hype — claim capability the product actually delivers
- No superlatives without evidence ("best", "fastest", "most advanced" — avoid unless sourced)

### German Engineering Confidence

- Hamburg origin and TUHH spin-off are credibility signals — use them
- Precision matters: "7-day self-sufficient battery", "≤120 min installation", "AI classification" — not vague claims
- "Made in Germany" — use as trust signal, not cliché. Pair with specific technical claims.

### B2B Direct (Not Consumer Marketing)

- No exclamation points unless quoting customer testimonials
- No "you won't believe" or clickbait phrasing
- Short paragraphs — logistics directors scan, not read
- Use bullet points for feature lists; narrative prose for use-case problems and outcomes

## Copy Transformation Examples

### Feature Description

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "Our system alerts you when theft is detected" | "Our system **detects and prevents** cargo theft — before loss occurs" |
| "Get notified of security events on your trailer" | "AI classifies events in real time and triggers an alarm chain to **deter** threats" |
| "Track your fleet with GPS" | "Secure your fleet with sensor-based prevention: GPS + motion + shock, classified by AI" |

### CTAs (Calls to Action)

| Avoid | Use Instead |
|-------|-------------|
| "Sign up" | "Beratung anfragen" (DE) / "Book a consult" (EN) |
| "Try for free" | Not applicable — Konvoi is B2B direct, quote-based |
| "Learn more" | "Mehr erfahren" — acceptable as secondary CTA only |
| "Get started" | Too SaaS / consumer — use "Beratung anfragen" |

### Problem Framing (Use-Case Pages)

Structure: **Cost anchor → Problem context → Konvoi approach**

Example (cargo theft):
> "Cargo theft costs the European logistics industry **€8 billion per year** (TAPA 2024). A single pharma shipment can represent **€1.2 million** in lost goods. Traditional tracking devices tell you where your trailer was stolen from — after the fact. KONVOI **prevents** theft by classifying threat patterns in real time and triggering a deterrence chain before cargo leaves the trailer."

## Localisation Notes

- **German (DE):** Default locale. Formal "Sie" — not informal "du". Use full German legal/regulatory terms when citing subsidies ("de-minimis-Förderung", not just "subsidy").
- **English (EN):** British English preferred for EU market. "organisation" not "organization". "colour" not "color". Exception: "KONVOI" is always all-caps in both locales.
- **KONVOI:** Always written in ALL CAPS. Never "Konvoi" in marketing copy (exception: code variables and file paths use camelCase as needed).

---

## Out of Scope for Brand Voice

- Legal copy (Impressum, Datenschutz) — neutral, factual, regulated language
- Technical documentation (sensor specs, API docs) — precise technical language, no marketing spin
- Internal communications — not governed by this guide
