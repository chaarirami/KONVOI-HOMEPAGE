## Organised Crime in European Freight Transport

Cargo theft is not a minor offence. According to TAPA (Transported Asset Protection Association), the European logistics industry suffers losses of over €8 billion per year — and the actual figure is likely considerably higher. Germany, France, Poland and the Benelux countries are particularly affected, as their motorway service areas and freight hubs are preferred targets for organised theft gangs.

The perpetrators operate professionally. Criminal networks systematically observe specific corridors and parking areas before striking. High-value cargo is especially at risk: pharmaceuticals, consumer electronics, tobacco goods, cosmetics and precious metals. A single pharmaceutical shipment can represent goods worth €1.2 million — a rewarding target for an organised gang that can execute a coordinated rip-off attack within minutes.

## How Attackers Exploit Security Gaps

The most common method is the rip-off attack: a group of perpetrators couples onto a parked trailer in the early hours — without waking the driver, without drawing attention. The tractor unit is gone before the driver realises anything is wrong. At motorway service areas and isolated parking spots, such operations can be carried out in under ten minutes.

There are also targeted attacks on stationary vehicles: thieves open trailer doors, remove partial loads and close them again — a method used particularly during long overnight stops with no supervision. In both cases, the critical vulnerability is the absence of early warning: damage is only discovered when the driver departs or arrives at the destination.

## Why Conventional GPS Tracking Always Comes Too Late

Traditional telematics solutions and GPS trackers answer the question "Where is my trailer?" — but this question only arises after the theft has already occurred. The notification is reactive: an alert is triggered when the trailer is already moving. By that point, perpetrators and cargo have a head start of minutes to hours.

The problem lies not in the GPS technology itself but in the approach: those who only react once the threat has already materialised are no longer protecting cargo — they are documenting the loss.

## The KONVOI Approach: Prevention Rather Than Reaction

KONVOI classifies threat patterns before damage occurs. The compact sensors on the trailer capture motion, shock and GPS data in real time. An AI-powered classification logic distinguishes between normal operation — such as regular coupling by the driver — and anomalous events such as unauthorised coupling, unusual shock patterns or GPS deviations.

When the system detects a threat pattern, it immediately triggers a multi-stage deterrence chain. This visible and audible response typically deters perpetrators before cargo is removed or a trailer is stolen. The principle: prevention through deterrence — not documentation after the fact.

For fleet operators handling high-value cargo, this delivers three concrete advantages: first, prevention of the direct loss from theft; second, reduced insurance premiums through documented preventive measures; and third, complete compliance documentation for customers, insurers and authorities. KONVOI provides both the protection and the proof.
