## High-Value Cargo: When a Single Trailer Can Threaten the Year's Result

The European transport market for high-value goods operates under sustained pressure. According to the TAPA 2024 report, cargo theft in Europe causes losses exceeding €8 billion per year — with a disproportionate share coming from the premium segment. Pharmaceuticals, electronics, precious metals, cosmetics and medical technology are the most frequently stolen categories, because the ratio of cargo value to transport volume is particularly attractive for criminals.

### The Risk Profile of This Segment

Organised theft rings operate with inside knowledge and precise timing. They identify high-value loads through freight documents, transfer points and unsecured communications — and strike preferentially at unguarded rest areas between 22:00 and 04:00. A single pharmaceutical trailer can cause losses of €1.2 million; for electronics shipments, a cargo value of €500,000 per semi-trailer is not unusual.

Cold chain interruptions on pharmaceutical transports create consequential costs that can exceed the direct cargo value — recall costs, liability towards recipients, regulatory consequences.

### How KONVOI Protects This Segment

KONVOI acts before the damage occurs. The system captures motion, shock and GPS patterns in real time and classifies them via AI as a threat or normal operation. As soon as an attack pattern is detected, KONVOI triggers an alarm chain — visible deterrence, driver notification, dispatch escalation — before cargo leaves the trailer.

For premium segments, this means: no post-incident investigation, but prevention. Not "where was my trailer stolen?" — but "the attack was stopped."
