## Refrigerated Transport: When Operational Disruption Costs More Than Theft

Temperature-controlled transport has a characteristic that sets it apart from other freight segments: it is not only the cargo value that is at risk — any uncontrolled operational disruption can break the cold chain and render the entire load unusable. For pharmaceutical shipments, this means regulatory consequences, recall costs and liability towards the recipient that can far exceed the original cargo value.

### The Risk Profile of This Segment

Refrigerated trailers are vulnerable on two levels. First, they are attractive theft targets — particularly for pharmaceuticals, high-value food products and cosmetics. Second, they depend on continuous diesel supply to power the refrigeration unit. In refrigerated transport, diesel theft is therefore not merely a fuel loss: a stolen tank often means unit failure, cold chain breach and complete loss of the load.

There are also heightened requirements for damage documentation: insurers demand comprehensive temperature and incident logs for cold chain interruptions. Missing documentation costs reimbursement claims.
